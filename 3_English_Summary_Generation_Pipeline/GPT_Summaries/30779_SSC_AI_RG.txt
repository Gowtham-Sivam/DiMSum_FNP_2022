National Express Group PLC's 2017 Annual Report highlights a strong year of growth, with revenue and profit both increasing significantly and being converted to strong free cash flow. The company's strategy of focusing on operational excellence, investing in technology, and disciplined acquisition and growth in new markets has been delivering results for several years. The Group's performance has been driven by growth across all of its internationally diversified businesses, with North America and ALSA having particularly strong years. The UK bus and coach businesses also managed to turnaround their challenges from the first half of 2017. National Express has invested in industry-leading on-demand scheduling technology at Ecolane, smart Lytx DriveCam safety cameras, and sophisticated customer pricing and payment methods. The disciplined acquisition strategy has brought high-quality new businesses into the Group and opened additional growth avenues. The Board has recommended a 10% increase in the final dividend, bringing the full year dividend to 13.51p, to be paid on 21 May 2018 to shareholders on the register on 27 April 2018.The Chairman's statement in the Annual Report 2017 of National Express Group PLC highlights the company's vision and values, which are aimed at generating shareholder value. The vision is to earn the lifetime loyalty of customers by consistently delivering frequent, high-performing public transport services that offer excellent value. The values underpinning this vision include excellence, safety, customer focus, people development, and community engagement. The report also outlines the company's business model, strategy and priorities, key performance indicators, and the environment in which it operates. It discusses the trends and factors impacting the development of the industry, the principal risks and uncertainties faced by the group, and how they are managed. Additionally, the report covers environmental, social, and employee issues, as well as the company's performance in 2017, including a review of the key factors impacting financial performance and divisional performance reviews for ALSA, North America, and the UK.National Express Group's strategy focuses on three strategic priorities: delivering operational excellence, deploying technology, and growing the business through acquisitions and market diversification. The company aims to be the safest, most reliable, convenient, and best value transport provider in the modes they operate. They utilize technology to raise customer and safety standards, drive efficiencies, and facilitate growth. They also look to grow their unique portfolio of international bus, coach, and rail businesses through selective bolt-on acquisitions and diversification into complementary markets. Performance highlights include EFQM five-star ratings for eligible businesses, improved customer satisfaction, and a strong retention rate in their North America school bus business. Future outlook includes targeting further passenger growth, launching new products and services, leveraging customer reputation to win new business, and continuing to embed best practices across the Group. Key performance indicators include passenger journeys, percentage of sales through digital channels, and return on capital employed (ROCE).In the 2017 Annual Report, National Express Group's Chief Executive highlights the company's focus on strong partnerships with local authorities, investment in technology, and operational excellence. The company has secured Department for Transport investment for new bus lanes on the Harborne to Birmingham city centre route and introduced state-of-the-art Platinum buses. They are also working on innovative transport technologies, such as autonomous electric vehicle projects in Madrid. The company has carried over 882 million passengers, up 1.2% on a continuing basis, and has seen high value for money scores. Safety remains a top priority, with a 74% reduction in harm since the inception of the Driving Out Harm programme. Technology investments include the roll-out of Lytx DriveCam smart safety cameras, contactless ticket machines, and improved digital presence. The company made nine acquisitions in 2017, with a strong pipeline of further opportunities in North America and ALSA. They also continue to target new growth opportunities through selected bidding.
