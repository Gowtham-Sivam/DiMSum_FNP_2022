The highlights of the financial report include a property revaluation surplus of £460m, up from £447m in 2016, an underlying EPS of 15.0p, consistent with 2016, and a diluted, adjusted NAV per share of 411p, an increase from 404p in 2016. The market value of investment properties also experienced growth.The Chairman's statement highlights the good operational and financial performance of intu properties plc in 2017. The company saw an increase in like-for-like net rental income of 0.5%, marking the third consecutive year of growth. Former BHS stores were substantially relet to quality retailers such as Next, Primark, and Uniqlo. A total of 217 long-term leases were signed, with 179 in the UK and 38 in Spain, delivering £38 million of annual rent at an average of 7% above the previous passing rent and in line with valuers' assumptions. The IFRS net assets attributable to owners of intu properties plc were £5,075m, up from £4,979m in 2016.In 2017, rent reviews settled at an average of 9% above previous passing rent, with occupancy stable at 96.1%. Footfall increased by 0.1%, outperforming the national ShopperTrak retail average which fell by 2.8%. Capital expenditure reached £184 million, including £63 million on the extension of intu Watford and £58 million on the acquisition of income-generating properties for future capital projects. The £180 million intu Watford extension is on target and on budget to open in October 2018, with pre-lets well advanced. The £72 million Nickelodeon-anchored leisure scheme at intu Lakeside was commenced, and the leisure line-up was completed by signing Hollywood Bowl, Puttshack, and Flip Out. Near-term committed and pipeline capital expenditure for the next three years is £562 million, with plans to start intu Trafford Centre (Barton Square – £72 million) and intu Broadmarsh, Nottingham (£81 million) projects in 2018. Unprompted awareness of the intu brand increased to 26% (2016: 22%), with prompted awareness increasing to 71% (2016: 63%). Net promoter score, a measure of customer service, remained consistent at around 70 throughout the year. intu Experiences, the dedicated promotions business, generated income of £22 million, equivalent to the rental income of the seventh-largest centre. intu.co.uk, the premium content publisher and shopping platform, delivered sales for retailers of £9 million, a 50% increase on 2016. The 2020 target of a 50% intensity reduction in carbon emissions was achieved three years ahead of plan. Madrid Xanadú, one of Spain’s top-10 shopping centres, was acquired for a headline price of €530 million, and TH Real Estate was introduced as a 50% joint venture partner. In Spain, 38 long-term leases were signed, delivering £2 million of annual rent at an average of 25% above previous passing rent. Occupancy remained stable at 97%, and footfall increased by 1%, despite disruption from the intu Asturias mall redevelopment, which has now successfully completed. A revaluation surplus of £98 million in Spain was achieved from intu Costa del Sol land (£74 million) and existing centres (£24 million), with intu Asturias up 11% and Puerto Venecia up 4%, driven by growth in rental values. At intu Costa del Sol, the proposed 230,000 sq m shopping resort development, approval of the General Plan of Torremolinos was a major step forward, with remaining consents expected in the coming months.
