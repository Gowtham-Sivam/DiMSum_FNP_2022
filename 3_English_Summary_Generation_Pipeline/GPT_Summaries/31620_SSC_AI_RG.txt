Sanderson Group reported a revenue increase to £21.56 million in 2017, up from £21.32 million in 2016. Pre-contracted recurring revenues also increased to £11.18 million, representing 52% of total revenue in the period, compared to £10.75 million in 2016. Operating profit rose to £3.90 million, and the company experienced strong cash generation with a net cash balance of £6.18 million at the end of September, well ahead of market expectations. The proposed final dividend increased by 11% to 1.55 pence per share, making the total dividend for the year 2.65 pence. Basic earnings per share increased by 18% to 5.2 pence. Sales order intake increased by 12% to £13.69 million, and the order book at the end of September stood at £5.79 million. Richard Mogg was appointed as Group Finance Director, and the company acquired Anisa Consolidated Holdings Limited, valued at £12 million.Sanderson Group plc is a profitable software and IT services business specializing in retail, manufacturing, wholesale distribution, and logistics sectors. Operating primarily in the UK and Ireland, the company provides enterprise systems designed to help customers run their businesses efficiently and profitably. Sanderson offers digital retail enterprise omni-channel retail technology, helping retailers bridge the gap between online and in-store shopping for a seamless customer experience. Solutions include in-store technology, back-office systems for processing sales and fulfilling orders, and mobile and e-commerce solutions for online operations. The company also provides manufacturing enterprise resource planning (ERP) software for general manufacturing, engineering, and food and drink processing businesses, streamlining processes and driving growth. Additionally, Sanderson offers industry-specific software and warehouse management systems for wholesale distribution, cash and carry, fulfillment, and logistics businesses, delivering sales growth, profitability, efficiency, and control. With a strong market position driven by innovation, Sanderson has over 550 customers, around 230 employees, and generates over £21 million in revenue.Sanderson Group, a software and IT services business specializing in digital retail technology and enterprise software, reported a revenue of £21.56 million for the year ended 30 September 2017, a slight increase from £21.32 million in 2016. Operating profit also increased to £3.90 million from £3.69 million in 2016. The company's gross margin remains high at 82%, with recurring revenue increasing to £11.18 million, representing 52% of total revenue. Sales order intake grew by 12% to £13.69 million, and the Group order book at 30 September 2017 was £5.79 million. Sanderson's strong cash-generative business model resulted in a cash balance of £6.18 million, well ahead of market expectations. The Board is proposing an 11% increase in the final dividend to 1.55 pence per ordinary share, making the total dividend paid in the year 2.65 pence per ordinary share. On 24 November, the Group announced the acquisition of Anisa Consolidated Holdings Limited for an enterprise value of £12 million, which is expected to provide incremental and synergistic market opportunities. The Group's strategy is to achieve sustained growth by continuing to build and develop the Sanderson business, with a focus on enhancing mobile and ecommerce solutions in Digital Retail and strengthening its proposition to customers in the Enterprise Division.In the Group Chief Executive's business review, Ian Newcombe highlights the expected rapid growth in Digital Retail and renewed impetus for growth from the Enterprise business, enabling the Group to meet its strategic targets. The target market for Sanderson products and services primarily comprises of SMEs (small and medium-sized enterprises), with a focus on fostering long-term customer relationships. This results in a high proportion of sales arising from pre-contracted recurring revenue, complemented by incremental sales to a strong, well-established, and growing customer base. Sanderson proprietary software is developed in anticipation of technological developments, often in conjunction with its large customer base, and is marketed and sold under a 'right to use' license. The Group's business solutions aim to provide customers with value for money IT systems, designed to offer cost-effective, timely, and tangible business benefits, enabling customers to increase revenue and achieve additional efficiencies. The Group continues to invest in the development of its software products and services, as well as in increasing its sales and marketing capacity and capability, with particular emphasis on businesses specializing in the UK food and drink processing and wholesale distribution sectors.
