The balanced approach to revenue management has driven returns, with like-for-like average occupancy for the year increasing by 1.4%. The like-for-like closing occupancy reached 75.0%, up 1.3 percentage points from 2016, and the like-for-like average storage rate for the year rose by 1.3% in CER.Safestore Holdings reported financial highlights for the year ended 31 October 2017, showing a 12.6% increase in revenue to £129.9m and a 13.6% increase in underlying EBITDA to £72.9m. The company acquired twelve Alligator stores for £56 million, which immediately enhanced earnings. Three new UK stores are in the pipeline, adding 146,000 sq ft of new space in London and Birmingham. In November 2017, contracts were exchanged for an 80,000 sq ft freehold site in Poissy, west of Paris. Safestore's debt refinancing in May 2017 resulted in approximately £3 million per annum finance cost savings on a pro forma basis. The group's loan-to-value ratio at 31 October 2017 was 36%, and the interest cover ratio was 6.7x.
