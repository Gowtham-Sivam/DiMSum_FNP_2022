In 2017, capital expenditure was increased to a record £115m, reflecting the strong increase in net contract gains. The company's approach to new business is disciplined and focuses on ensuring that it generates good returns on investment. The success is underpinned by the ability to deliver an attractive brand line-up and innovative bespoke concepts for clients and customers. SSP Group continues to invest in its people, strengthening central, regional, and local teams around the world. The Board also proposes a special dividend of approximately £100m, reflecting confidence in the future of the business and the desire to maintain an efficient balance sheet. SSP Group remains committed to operating sustainably in its markets and responsibly managing environmental and social issues that are material to the business. The Group is continuing to invest in the development of the business and to bring new brands and concepts to clients and customers. Strong financial results were achieved, with underlying operating profit increasing by 27.0% (on a constant currency basis) to £162.9m, and total revenue increasing by 11.7% on a constant currency basis. Like-for-like growth in the air sector has been stronger than the rail sector, driven by increasing passenger numbers in most markets. Net contract gains were 6.0% in the year, with strong contributions from North America and the Rest of the World. Significant new openings at Chicago Midway and JFK T7 airports in North America, and in Hong Kong and China in the Rest of the World, have contributed to this strong performance. The pipeline of new contracts is encouraging, with significant new contracts won at airports in Seattle, Los Angeles, Boston, and Cebu in the Philippines. The strong operating margin improvement of 50 bps reflects the like-for-like sales growth and further encouraging progress on strategic initiatives. The Group delivered strong free cash flow of £89.0m, after investing £115.0m in capital expenditure (excluding capital contributions from partners), which was a £19.1m increase on the prior year. Reported net debt fell from £317.4m to £262.2m, driven by the free cash flow of £89.0m, net of the dividend payment of £29.0m. The Group is proposing a special dividend of approximately £100m, accompanied by a proposed share consolidation, reflecting confidence in the future of the business and the desire to maintain an efficient balance sheet. The new financial year has started in line with expectations, and the pipeline of new contracts is encouraging.
